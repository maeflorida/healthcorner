10 Stress Reduction Tips

Limit self-judgment
Talk kindly to yourself, turning off the “negative tape player.”
Get help from others
Tapping into our support networks helps us to feel understood, capable, and nurtured.
Express your feelings
If you can’t discuss your feelings with your support network, express them some other way through journal, poem, or compose a letter that is never mailed.
Work to reduce or manage the impact of major stressors in your life
One way of monitoring your stress level and identifying sources of stress is to keep a daily stress log.
Do just one thing at a time
When working, focus on one thing at a time.
Know and accept your limits
Learning to say no is an important part of reducing stress and will help you focus on what’s really important.
Get a good night’s sleep
It’s well documented that students who regularly get a good night’s sleep perform significantly better than sleep-deprived students
Learn and practice relaxation techniques
Relaxation lowers blood pressure, respiration, and pulse rates.
Know that good nutrition and exercise are your friends
General health and stress resistance can be enhanced by regular exercise, a diet rich in a variety of whole grains, vegetables, and fruits, and by avoiding excessive alcohol, caffeine, and tobacco.
Make it a priority to do something low pressure and enjoyable
Get crafty (art, scrapbooking, writing/journaling), listen to music, get physical (walk, run, dance, do yoga), get outside, or just give yourself a few minutes off from what you are doing to do simply nothing.

General First Aid Treatment Tips for Common Injuries

1- Cut or scrapes:
  • Wash the area with lukewarm water
  • Gently pat the area dry
  • Use soap if the cut occured due to dirst or animal scratch
  • Now wipe the blood with a clean cotton
  • Apply over the counter antibiotic like polysporin and neosporin or bacitracin
  • Cover with a bandage or gauze
  • Apply direct pressure to control bleeding
  • If the wound is deep and bleeding does not stop, then rush to ER immediately
  • Use antibiotic cream everyday after wiping the area clean
  • Use new bandages daily if needed, or leave open for quick dry
  • For swelling, pus or reddness see your healthcare provider immediately
  • Use SPF 30 creams on newly healed skin as they get sunburned easily
2- Painful and red mosquito bite:
  • Clean the area with soap and water at least 3-5 times a day
  • Do not scratch with your fingernails as your fingernails bacteria may infect the site
  • Use an antibacterial ointment for reddness and swelling
  • If you see flu like symptoms visit your healthcare provider immediately
3- Nosebleeds:
Immediate first aid for a nosebleed:
  • Sit them down in slightly leaning down pose
  • Pinch soft and fleshy part of noe
  • Breath through mouth and also spit out the blood if any
  • Check their consciousness and blood pressure
  • Keep temperature cool and apply ice packs on neck
  • Apply a topical antiseptic if the bleeding stops with general first aid else visit accident and emergency centre.
  • For recurrent nosebleeds visit an ear, nose and throat specialist.
4- Burns:
Immediate first aid for a burn:
  • Keep the person in cool temperature but do not over cool
  • Look for their breathing and other injuries
    •  
  • If the clothes are burning roll over on ground
  • Remove clothing and jewellery and leave the parts stuck on skin
  • Apply running water for at least half an hour or more to clean and soothe the burn
  • Use burn cream or spray after patting the area dry
  • If the burn is thrid degree or looks very bad cover it with burn wrap or a plastic sheet
  • Rush to ER immediately
  • You may also use an anti-inflammatory for pain reliefs
5- Seizures:

Seizues are common and may be recurring. First aid here aims to keep the person safe on ground when they experience seizure.

  • Place a pillow under head and allow the person to lie down
  • Check for the history by any jewelry or card they may have carried along
  • Do not feed ot hold the person straight
  • When seizure stops check the airway and ensure they are breathing properly else call 911 immediately
  • Monitor the person and if seizure lasts more than 5 minutes call 911
6- Vasovagal syncope:
Immediate first aid for vasovagal syncope:
  • Lay the patient down, flat on a ground
  • Check their breating and open their mouth to help them breath
  • Do not feed them anything
  • Raise the legs above ground to ensure blood circulation in brain
  • Open and untie their clothes specailly around waist and neck
  • Consciousness is generally regained in less than a few minutes
  • But in cases when person is unconscious for more than 5 minutes visit ER immediately
6- Broken tooth:

Broken or avulsed tooth can be a result of an accident or injury. This can not be healed but re-implantation can be done.

Immediate first aid for a broken permanent tooth:
  • Clean your hands and wear gloves
  • Do not try to pick or move the tooth
    •  
  • Use milk or saline water to rinse the tooth
  • Just hold the tip on the tooth and try to implant back in place
  • Ask the person to chew on gauze to fit it well in place
    •  
  • Refer to a dentist on urgent

General first aid tips for all injuries:

It is important to prevent any further damage after an injury so follow the below steps to treat the condition with general first aid:

  1. Protect from further injury in the affected area
  2. Rest at least 48-72 hours post injury
  3. Use ice packs on injury
  4. Drink plenty of fluids
  5. Alleviate the injured area above the level of heart for the first 48-72 hours
  6. Avoid heat, alcohol, rigorous exercise or massage fir first 48 hours to avoid swelling
  7. Keep a first aid box handy at home all the time

The above compilation on first aid for different types of injuries is to help you attend to the conditions in utmost helpful ways before reaching out to the ER. Most of these common injuries require no additional hospital visit and can be attended at home itself with these general first aid tips. But visiting a healthcare provider for any moderate to svere injury must be top priority.
